Executive summary

A scheduled maintenance for our ISP in NYC, which we were not notified of, resulted in a multi-hour outage affecting multiple sites and nearly all employees.

Background

NYC's internet access is provided by Lumen ( https://www.lumen.com/ ), with a secondary ISP available via manual fail-over via GTT ( https://www.gtt.net ).

Incident Description

A scheduled, essential maintenance began on 2022-05-24 12:00am ET. The maintenance completed, but service was not restored.

Analysis

A combination of internal and external failures led to us not knowing about scheduled maintenance for a major office. Subsequent external failures led to a prolonged outage affecting nearly all users worldwide. The process for recovering from those failures required skilled personnel and an advanced understanding of internal and external configurations and dependencies.

Failing over to a secondary ISP was a very manual process that required physical and configuration changes that were not, and likely could not be, automated.

The impact of this outage will lead to large-scale changes in the way that systems interconnect and our response to failures.

Detection

How quickly was the problem detected?

The issue was detected immediately by monitoring systems, which alerted IT personnel within five minutes of the outage.

How was the problem detected?

The problem was detected by LibreNMS monitoring, which created a PagerDuty alert.

How can detection be improved?

Detection was immediate and worked as designed.

Response

How quickly did we respond once detected?

We didn't respond to the incident until 19 minutes after the outage started. This could have been better, but ultimately wouldn't have changed the time-to-resolution.

Did we adequately communicate to our users?

Communication to end users happened via the IT Support Hotline soon after the outage was confirmed.

However, our Incident Management Procedure says that we should have also communicated to the entire company via email with more information as soon as we understood the outage and impact. This was not done until 07:38 ET.

If not, how can this be improved?

For an outage of this duration (especially overnight in the US), we should always consider, where possible, transitioning the technical and communications roles to team members working around the world.
In this case in particular, the responsibilities of following up with Lumen support and communicating the status of the outage could have been handed off.

Mitigation

What went well that helped minimize the impact of this problem?

The impact of this problem was far-reaching, impacting sites globally as well as our cloud resources.
One thing that helped mitigate the impact was the global availability of Appgate services, which were useful once DNS resolvers were set to use DNS servers located outside of the NYC office.
Having a second ISP available for failover, even if only manually, gave us the ability to restore services without depending on a single vendor.

The remediation team also did a great job of and consistently used 'safelist' in the chat room. This didn't have a direct bearing on the problem but does still have a positive effect on our culture of inclusiveness and warrants recognition.

What can be done to minimize the impact if a problem like this recurs?

We need to put deliberate effort into minimizing the reliance on any single site or ISP for global services.

Avoidance

What can we do in the future to avoid a problem like this?

Describe any ideas you have (even potentially impractical ones) for assuring problems like this one don't happen again. Complete avoidance is probably not an attainable goal but shoot for at least highly unlikely.
