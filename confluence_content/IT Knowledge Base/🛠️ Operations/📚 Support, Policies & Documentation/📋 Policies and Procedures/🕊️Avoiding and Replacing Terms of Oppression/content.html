INLINE

IT will work to avoid using terms, especially in naming, that evoke negative associations with oppression or convey bias.  The primary examples here are "master/slave" and "whitelist/blacklist".

Background

For years various segments of the technology industry has adopted widespread use of terms that evoke or hearken to bigoted or oppressive views or relationships. Language can and does have the effect, over time, of normalizing such use but doesn't erase these negative associations and may in fact serve to entrench them further. Many, if not most, of these may have arisen without malice but the negative and cumulative effects of perpetrating this shouldn't be ignored.

For a more in-depth treatment of why this is significant please see: https://blog.carbonfive.com/problematic-terminology-in-open-source/

Policy statement

Schrödinger IT rejects the use of language and naming which conveys or evokes associations with bigotry and oppression. Choosing instead to replace these terms--both formally and informally--with more neutral language wherever possible.

Action plan

Policy statements are good but must be backed up with a commitment to action. So we will undertake the following in an effort to right this wrong.

  1. We will take a stand on the issue. We are doing that here and in our subsequent actions.
  2. We will will compile and maintain a and possible replacements and add this to our collection of naming conventions and practices.
  3. We will in the course of our work be on the lookout for such language represented in our environment and change it.
  4. We will be vigilant for such language as we evaluate new products and software for purchase and ask our current and prospective vendors to address and change when we find this.

  5. Where we find we cannot eliminate these terms because the vendor or supplier can't or won't we'll apply a banner or warning to our documentation acknowledging that fact and underscoring our opposition to it.


Places to check for names and terms

  • Account names in directories (AD/LDAP/Okta)
