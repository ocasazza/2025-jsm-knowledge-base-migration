When submitting a ticket for an issue it is important to classify the urgency and impact correctly so it receives the correct level of attention from the team you are escalating to.

When these have been entered accurately they will show up in the cue with the correct priority and enable the team to address the most business critical issues first.

Urgency indicating the time sensitivity of the issue and Impact indicating the scale of the issue.

Another important thing to remember is CONTEXT when filling out the issue description.

Without proper context we will not be able to accurately evaluate whether the priority is correct or needs to be adjusted.

Example

You are submitting a ticket for your external webcam not working.

Now with no further context to that issue I would probably put that as

Low Impact, Medium Urgency.

Low Impact because it is only affecting you

Medium Urgency because of how much we use video conferencing and that interfaces with other people

Now adding in the context of,

My laptop webcam doesn’t work. I have no alternative means of attending meetings AND I have a meeting with the board in 1/2 an hour.

The priority, obviously would be changed to High Urgency and High Impact

High Urgency due to the time constraints

High Impact due to the business critical nature of the meeting

Now for a less drastic example.

Let’s say you are submitting a ticket because you would prefer a different USB-C hub than the one you were issued.

This would obviously be a Low Impact, Low Urgency issue.

Low Impact because it is only affecting you

Low urgency because it does not affect your ability to work

Now with the additional context of,

You've ask around and 1/3 of the company doesn’t like the USB-C hubs they were issued.

This is a perfect example of a High Impact, Low Urgency Issue that we should look into.

High Impact because it is affecting a Large number of people

Low urgency because it is not affecting their ability to work
