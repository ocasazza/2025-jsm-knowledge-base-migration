MHG - Friedrich

Friedrich is the bigger conference room and often used for bigger meetings and all-hands meetings. It is equipped with 2 large displays.

MHG - Heinrich

Heinrich is the smaller conference room and often used for smaller meetings and candidate interviews. It is equipped with only 1 display.

Default Settings

  • Before you begin make sure the TV is on and not on Power Savings Mode/Sleeping.

  • Remotes may run out of batteries, replace them as needed. Always keep the iPad plugged in.

  • TV Input should always be on “ HDMI 3 / ARC ”. Don’t change that unless you know what you're doing.

  • The system doesn’t auto-switch to the desired input source.

  • You have to open the compartment and select the correct input from the panel in fig. 1 and fig 4.

  • You can use the wall input switcher in fig 9 to switch the input to your desired source. In case the wall input is not working as needed, you can use the input switcher panel inside the compartment.

Google Meet

  • First switch to “ Google Meet ” from the Meeting Switcher in the compartment wall input switcher.

    • (See Fig 1,3 and 4).

  • Press the “ OK ” button on the Google Meet Remote to wake the Chromebox from sleep mode. Sometimes the Chromebox hardware goes into sleep mode to conserve energy. If you only see the Google Meet logo fading in and out on the screen it’s on sleep mode.

  • Use the table microphones (fig. 7) to control mute/unmute for the room. Use the Google Meet Remote(fig. 6) to manage audio level, camera on/off, turn on chat/attendees list display on the right side of the screen, etc

  • Don’t change the default audio/video devices.

    • Default Microphone: Logitech Rally Audio: USB Audio:0,0

    • Default Speaker: Logitech Rally Audio: USB Audio:0,0

    • Default Video: Logitech Rally Camera (046d:0881)

  • The video quality could be poor if there is too much bright light coming through the windows.

Zoom Rooms

  • First switch to the “ Zoom Rooms” from the Meeting Switcher in the compartment.

    • (See Fig 1,3 and 4).

  • Use the iPad to start or join the scheduled Zoom meeting/webinar

  • You can either use the iPad or desk microphone to mute/unmute. You can do a lot more controlling from the iPad. Use as needed.

  • Default settings are set and locked in the iPad. Don’t change the settings.

Laptops

  • First switch to the “ Tisch USB-C ” from the Meeting Switcher in the compartment. (See Fig 1,3 and 4).

  • Then plug the USB-C from the desk to your computer.

  • Make sure that the USB-C/HDMI Switcher under the table by the side of the window is powered on (with a red light) and input selected with orangish yellow light.

    • See Fig 8

  • Select the audio device to output sound from the computer if that is desired. On Mac, you can click the volume button on the top right area and choose Logitech Rally Audio . Not ITE HDMI and also not MUC - Ludwig (Apple TV icon).

  • On Windows, you can do so by clicking on the volume icon on the taskbar bottom right corner and selecting Logitech Rally Audio .

General Tips

  • After the end of every meeting, make sure you exit the meeting. (End the Google Meets by clicking on the “Leave call” sign, end Zoom meeting from the iPad controller, and close the program that has been using the camera while projecting from laptops).

    • This enables the camera to sleep and be ready for the next event. If a previous meeting is not exited, the camera may look up but not work for the current meeting.

  • Make sure all systems are powered on, batteries are not dead, iPad is fully charged and plugged in.

  • TV source input should generally be in HDMI 3.

  • You can control volume in multiple ways and which means you may have to check them all. TV Remote control, Meeting specific volume controls, etc.
