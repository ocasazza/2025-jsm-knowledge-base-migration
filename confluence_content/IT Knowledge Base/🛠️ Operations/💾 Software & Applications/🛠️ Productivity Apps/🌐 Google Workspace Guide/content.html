Module 2: Gmail and Google Calendar

Gmail Setup

  1. Compose an Email :

    • Click 'Compose' on the top left side.

    • Enter recipient's email, subject, and your message.

    • Explore 'Formatting options' (A) for text styling.

    • Click 'Send' once done.

  2. Create Labels :

    • Go to 'Settings' (gear icon) > 'See all settings'.

    • Click on the 'Labels' tab.

    • Scroll down to 'Labels' and click 'Create new label'.

    • Name your label (e.g., 'Project X') and click 'Create'.

  3. Apply Filters :

    • Go to 'Settings' > 'Filters and Blocked Addresses'.

    • Click 'Create a new filter'.

    • Enter criteria (e.g., emails from a specific person).

    • Choose what to do with filtered emails (e.g., apply a label).

Google Calendar Scheduling

  1. Create an Event :

    • In Google Calendar, click on the desired date.

    • Add event title, time, and details.

    • Click 'Save'.

  2. Invite Attendees :

    • While creating an event, add attendees' emails in 'Guests'.

    • Click 'Send' to send calendar invites.

Module 3: Google Drive - Storage and Collaboration

Upload, Share, and Collaborate

  1. Upload a Document :

    • Click 'New' > 'File upload'.

    • Select a file from your computer.

    • Once uploaded, find it in 'My Drive'.

  2. Share and Collaborate :

    • Right-click the file and select 'Share'.

    • Enter a colleague's email.

    • Choose their permission level (Edit, View, Comment).

    • Click 'Send'.

Module 4: Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides

Document Creation and Collaboration

  1. Create and Import in Google Docs :

    • Open Google Docs, click on '+ Blank'.

    • To import, click 'File' > 'Open' > 'Upload', then choose a Word file.

    • Edit as needed, noting differences in layout or features.

  2. Collaborate on a Spreadsheet :

    • Open Google Sheets, start a new sheet.

    • Share it with a colleague (as above in Google Drive).

    • Enter data in cells and use the 'Chat' feature to communicate.

Module 5: Google Meet and Additional Tools

Host a Video Call and Create a Survey

  1. Google Meet Video Call :

    • In Google Calendar, create an event and click 'Add Google Meet video conferencing'.

    • Invite participants by adding their emails in 'Guests'.

    • During the meeting, click 'Present now' for screen sharing.

  2. Google Forms Survey :

    • Open Google Forms, click '+ Blank'.

    • Create questions using different formats (multiple choice, short answer).

    • Click 'Send' to share the survey link via email or copy the link.

Follow-Up Activities

  • Experiment with Advanced Features : After getting comfortable with the basics, encourage exploring advanced features like conditional formatting in Sheets or master slides in Slides.

  • Create a Sample Project : Assign a task where the trainee needs to use multiple Google Workspace tools. For example, prepare a project proposal using Google Docs, organize project data in Google Sheets, and present the proposal using Google Slides.

Additional Practical Exercises

Gmail Advanced Usage

  • Organize Inbox with Filters and Labels : Create a system to automatically sort incoming emails. For example, emails from a specific client can be labeled "Client X" and starred.

  • Schedule an Email : Compose an email and schedule it to be sent at a later time by clicking the dropdown next to 'Send' and selecting 'Schedule send'.

Google Calendar Efficient Planning

  • Set Up Recurring Meetings : Schedule a weekly team meeting by setting the event to repeat weekly.

  • Create Personal Appointment Slots : Under 'My calendars', click on a time slot and select 'Appointment slots' for others to book meetings with you.

Google Drive Organization Skills

  • Create a Shared Drive : Create a drive for team collaboration, setting appropriate access levels for different members.

  • Use 'Starred' Feature : Star important documents for quick access in the future.

Google Docs, Sheets, and Slides Collaboration

  • Commenting and Suggesting Edits : In a shared document, highlight text and add a comment. Switch to 'Suggesting' mode to propose edits.

  • Real-Time Collaboration Experiment : Work simultaneously on a document with a colleague, observing real-time changes and chat functionality.

Google Meet Effective Communication

  • Record a Meeting : Start a Google Meet call, click on the three dots for more options, and select 'Record meeting' (ensure company policy allows recording).

  • Use Breakout Rooms : During a larger meeting, use breakout rooms to divide participants into smaller groups for discussions.

Google Forms Data Collection

  • Analyze Survey Responses : After collecting responses from a survey, use the 'Responses' tab to analyze the data graphically.
