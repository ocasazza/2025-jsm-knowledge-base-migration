Using the Whiteboard feature that Zoom has rolled out can be a game-changer during important meetings.

The feature allows participants of the meeting to access and write different notes, create drawings and collaborate more closely.

To start using the Whiteboard feature in meeting, you must "Join" or "Create a meeting", as you would normally do to start a Zoom call.

After that, you can look in the tools below the meeting screen and you'll see a "Whiteboards" icon to the right of the toolbar.



After clicking the button, you'll see a screen like the one below:



This is where you'll get to create and write notes with whomever you're in the call with.

You might notice the toolbar on the left hand side. You are given easy access to all of the different tools you can use during the call.

In order, we'll go through what some of them are used for:


  • Select - In which you can choose to select to "grab" and drag the page, positioning it for whatever you might need.
  • Draw - You can select the pen or highlighter and pick how thick the mark will be (choices are Thin, Regular and Thick)
  • Shape - You can pick and place shapes on your Whiteboard, whether you want to write in them or shade them in with different colors






  • Line- You can produce a line (Arrow, Double Arrow, Line) for anything you might want to highlight in your presentation or drawings
  • Text- You can open a text box and write whatever you need into it
  • Sticky Note - You can create your sticky notes for the whiteboard and resize them however you like
  • Templates- Picking and choosing templates to fit your project ( )
  • Image- Choose any image you want from your computer and paste it on the board.
  • More tools - Which include Table, Mind Maps, Kanban, Card etc.
  • Erase - Erases any of your creations
  • Redo and Undo - Used for anything that may get erased accidentally or drawn accidentally


You may be able to add additional buttons, but that's for more advanced use cases.


Furthermore, You are able to share your Whiteboard with others.




You can type in a C ontact Name , Channel or Email Address of the person who you want to join. You can delegate their access level in the drop now menu next to their name and you can also control who sees the link, pictured below.



If you click the chatbox in the top right corner, you can leave a comment on the board in case something needs clarification or if you'd like to let your collaborator know something:



If you click the ellipses to the right of the box, you'll see options to Report a Whiteboard, Lock the board and Move to Trash , in case any of those will apply after you complete your board.


Now that the basics are covered, you're ready to use the Zoom Whiteboard!
