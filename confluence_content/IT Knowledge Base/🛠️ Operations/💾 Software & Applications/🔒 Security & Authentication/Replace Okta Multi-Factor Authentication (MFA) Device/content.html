This document is intended for users who need to replace their MFA device to connect to Okta.

Background

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) increases your account security when signing in to Okta and other applications you use, and SYSMGR recommends configuring multiple verification methods (phone and laptop fingerprint reader or security key, for example) when setting up your Okta account.
This will, for example, allow you to to still sign into Okta using your laptop fingerprint reader or a security key as the second factor if your phone is not available (it's broken, or you've switched to a new phone and need to enroll it).  Having multiple verification methods configured will help you if you have lost or replaced a phone configured with the Okta Verify app or a physical security key, or if your MFA device is not being properly recognized.

Some restrictions apply if you do not have a second method of authenticating.

Currently, there are two options for MFA when signing into Okta at Schrodinger:

  • Okta Verify app for your mobile device: Install Okta Verify

  • Use a hardware security key or the built-in fingerprint reader on your MacBook Pro.

    • For instructions on setting up your laptop's fingerprint reader for use with Okta, see ITLC Authenticate Okta with Built-in Fingerprint Readers on Laptops

      • Note: Some laptops may not have a Biometric reader therefore a Hardware Security key will be required as a second option when authenticating.

    • Please open an SYSMGR ticket if you would like to request a Hardware Security Key.

Reasons to reset:

  • Reset Okta Verify if you are in one of these situations:

    • You wiped or reset your current device after you already set up Okta Verify.

    • You want to set up your current Okta Verify account on a new device.

    • You're stuck in an authentication loop - the browser instructs you to check the Okta Verify app but the app doesn't receive a push notification or display a one-time code.

    • You can't sign in to your account even if you enter the correct passcode from the app or approve a push notification sent to your device.

    • You keep receiving a verification code error in the Okta Verify app.

Prerequisites

  1. A second method to authenticate into Okta must be configured. (Ex. Biometric/Fingerprint-reader or Hardware Security Key.
    If you do not have this set-up please reach out to IT-Help for assistance.

Instructions

Remove Existing MFA App / Device

  1. From a browser, sign in to your Okta account. If you can't sign in with your existing Okta Verify app, or with another authentication factor, contact IT.

  2. Go to Settings . ( Settings page from the menu in the upper-right corner.)

3. Click the Edit Profile button.

4. Enter your password when prompted.

5.  Scroll down to the Extra Verification section.


6. Next to Okta Verify, click Remove . This removes the existing Okta Verify registration from your device, which is mandatory before you can reset Okta Verify on your current device or on a new one.

7. When prompted to confirm removal, click Yes

Enroll Your Mobile Device Running Okta Verify

1. In the Okta Verify row, click Set up to reset your enrollment or enroll a new device.

2. On the Setup Okta Verify page, select your device type and click Next.

3. After selecting your device type a QR code should appear on the screen.

4. On your mobile device, download the Okta Verify app from the app store (Apple or Google Play) and install it.

  1. Open Okta Verify on your device and follow the instructions.

  2. Tap Add account . When prompted, allow notifications and access to your camera .

  3. If the following screen appears on your browser, click Finish . (This screen appears only in some cases).

5. Completely Log out of Okta and verify that you are now able to use your mobile device as your second form of authentication.
