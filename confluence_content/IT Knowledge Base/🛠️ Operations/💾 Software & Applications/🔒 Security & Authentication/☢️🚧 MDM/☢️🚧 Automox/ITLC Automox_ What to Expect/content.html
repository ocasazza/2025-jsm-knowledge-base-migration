In an effort to maintain end-user laptops and desktops updated IT has implemented and installed the Automox agent on all machines. Automox should aid Windows, Mac and Linux machine updates by downloading them in the background and applying them on a schedule.


INLINE

You should not have to do anything on your end, but you can expect the following Patch policy and notifications to occur below.

Patch Policy Schedule

Monday - Thursday 5 pm local time


If your device fails to apply the scheduled patch at the designated time, it will automatically attempt to install the patch during its next check-in.

Can I defer reboot?

You will have the option to defer in 1, 4 and 8 hour intervals. It can only be deferred up to 3 times after you will be forced to update.


If you close the notification or do not see it this will count as deferral.

Example:

The deferral time options allowed are 1, 4, and 8 hours for reboots. If the user does not see the reboot notification message and the notification timeout has passed, then the 8-hour deferral is automatically consumed. The next reboot notification is sent in 8 hours until the user either acknowledges the notification, or runs out of deferrals.


*If you are out of deferrals Automox will force a reboot after 15 minutes


Notifications

Automox will notify you once updates are ready to be applied you can expect notifications as follows

Not all updates will require a reboot.


While Automox will prompt you to reboot, some applications may pause your reboot as they wait for your confirmation to exit or save (ie iterm or office).


Linux users will not see a notification window and updates should run in the background.

Based on which system you are running you might see a process named "amagent" this is the Automox agent which is installed on your machine.
