This is a public-facing Zoom documentation for End-Users.


Purpose

Zoom at Schrödinger is used as the default conferencing solution for:

  • Communication that involves multiple internal employees when Google Meet is not the preferred solution. e.g Weekly/Quarterly All Hands
  • Or communication that involves parties outside Schrödinger.


Zoom replaced Cisco WebEx for the above purposes. The shutdown date for WebEx at Schrodinger was June 2020.


You are free to use Google Meet for your regular stand-ups, 1-1s etc instead of Zoom.

Setup and Tips

If you are a regular user of Zoom, we recommend the following optional tools to make scheduling and attending meetings easier.

  • Zoom Client for Meetings


General Advice
  • Always stay signed-in . If you use a personal Zoom account, re-sign in with Schrodinger when done using it.
    • If you don't sign in, you'll be stuck in the Waiting Room till someone lets you in.
  • Don’t change settings you are not sure about.
  • Change settings only after reviewing the .
  • Ask urgent questions in the IT Support Hotline . Create an accompanying Jira ticket if it needs investigation or follow up.


When attending meetings
  • Launch Zoom from your Okta dashboard and then click the meeting link from either your Zoom Desktop application or Google Calendar.
  • Stay signed in to your Zoom desktop application.
  • External users (and employees who are not signed-in) will be put into the Waiting Room. Hosts and Co-hosts will be able to let them in individually or en masse.
  • If you are not signed-in, you might be prompted to enter your name when you join meetings. In such cases, make sure you use your Schrodinger full name.
  • Be mindful of the name and profile picture in your non-Schrodinger zoom account when you join Schrodinger Zoom meetings.
  • The solution to both is being signed in with your Schrodinger Zoom account.


When setting up meetings
  • Be aware that Waiting Room is now the default security method. You can optionally add passcodes for additional security. Passcodes are now disabled by default .

Y ou can only switch from Waiting Room to Passcodes if you schedule your meeting via the Zoom application or Zoom website, not via the Google Calendar event. Unless you to always include one.

  • Allow participants to join before host is now enabled for everyone. Please confirm your personal settings are set to allow participants to join before the host, as your settings may be different.
  • If you host Zoom settings frequently, please look at additional .

FAQ

I'm always put in the Waiting Room when I join internal meetings, why is that?

Our now default security setting is Waiting Room. All users who have signed in to their Zoom client automatically bypass the Waiting Room. If you haven't signed in, then you will be thrown into the Waiting Room till you get admitted. Passcodes can be used as additional or replacement security method.


I see non-standard names in the list of users who joined my internal meeting, why is that?

Users who are not signed in can enter temporary names when they join the meeting. If you think/suspect the user should not be in your meetings, the meeting host has the ability to throw them into the Waiting Room  or remove them from the meeting. Users who are signed in will always have their proper preferred First name + Last name and can not change it.


I'm getting 40 min limit warnings, what is the difference between a Basic and a Licensed account?

A Basic account is generally used for joining meetings while Licensed accounts are used to host meetings and webinars. Almost everyone in Schrodinger has a Licensed account. If you're missing a feature you need, please reach out to IT.


What will happen to Schrodinger WebEx?

We are retiring WebEx and it will no longer be in use at the company.


What happens if our external collaborator is not allowed to use Zoom or Google Meet?

If your collaborator is sanctioned from using Google Meet or Zoom by their IT Policy, then you can ask them to host the meeting using their preferred tool. If there is a business need or recurring use case, we can provide you with a MS Teams license.


How do I run Zoom Meetings in the conference rooms?

Use the conference room laptop to sign in to Zoom. If you are the host, start hosting the Meeting/Webinar. If you are just joining a Zoom Meeting/Webinar created by others, fill in the Meeting ID and Password to join the meeting. Make sure the Audio and Video Sources are set correctly. It’s always a good idea to do the little test before joining.


Zoom is not working as expected or is misbehaving, what should I do?

You can always check the Service Status Page at https://status.zoom.us/ to see if the problem is on their side. If all is fine, please message the IT Support Hotline or create a JIRA ticket.


How can I Schedule a Meeting to a time that is not in the drop-down while using the Google Calendar Plug In?

You can type in the exact time you want to schedule the meeting or Webinar instead of using the drop-down.


I created a Meeting instead of a Webinar, can I convert the Meeting to a Webinar?

Yes, go to the Meeting and configure that in the settings, at the bottom of the page.


Can I make my settings stick instead of configuring the settings for each Meeting individually?

Yes, sign in to Zoom in the web interface and head to Personal →  Settings on the left side.
