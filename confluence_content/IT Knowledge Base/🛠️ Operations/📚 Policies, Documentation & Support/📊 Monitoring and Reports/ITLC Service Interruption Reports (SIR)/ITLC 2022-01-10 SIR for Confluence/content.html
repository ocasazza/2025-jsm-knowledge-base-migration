Executive summary

There were problems with accessing the Confluence service after the host VM, gcp-confluence-lv01, was updated and rebooted around 9:45pm Eastern on . This turned out to be due to two issues:

a stale PID blocking the Confluence service from starting after the reboot (resolved by another reboot)

an IP address change for gcp-confluence-lv01.  The IP address was updated in external DNS (nsone.net) and internal DNS (pdx-ad-wv01) to resolve this.


Background

Our weekly updates still listed the old VM for Confluence, aws-confluence-lv01.  The current VM for Confluence is in GCP, and is gcp-confluence-lv01.  It hadn't been moved into the update rotation yet, and when I logged it, it hadn't been rebooted in 60+ days. I ran apt-get update and apt-get upgrade and rebooted, and that's when the issues started.

Incident Description

After the reboot, I tried to access confluence.schrodinger.com directly and from my Okta dashboard.  After some time, I'd get redirected to https://confluence.schrodinger.com/errors.jsp with a page of errors (a copy is posted here ). Trying to restart the Confluence service didn't help.  (In retrospect, another VM reboot would probably have resolved this part of the issue, due to a stale PID file.)  Since I'd done an update, my first thought was perhaps some incompatibility had been introduced by the package changes.  After looking at the errors on https://confluence.schrodinger.com/errors.jsp, I incorrectly thought it might be a database connection issue.  I asked the sysmgr chat room for advice. As it was late, I notified end-users via chat and email of the issue and that it might not be resolved till the morning.

rebooted again, getting past the stale PID issue.  Then we ran into a new issue, which found was due to a change in IP address for gcp-confluence-lv01 vs what we had in DNS. After updated external DNS, then later updated internal DNS, the issue was fully resolved and the all-clear was sent to users via email and chat.

Chronology

Eastern time

9:49pm On gcp-confluence-lv01, ran apt-get update; apt-get upgrade. Rebooted.

10:00pm Pinged in the sysmgr chat room to indicate Confluence did not seem to be working properly after the reboot and trying to restart the service.  Created System JIRA ac6def98-2140-30c3-8103-4b52b8b31135 SYSMGR-61927 and notified users via the IT Hotline and an email to all domain users.

11:00pm Updated sysmgr chat that the issue seemed to be related to a database connection.  Added a comment to System JIRA ac6def98-2140-30c3-8103-4b52b8b31135 SYSMGR-61927 for end users to indicate the issue might not be resolved till the next day.

8:30am began investigating.  Sometime during this investigation, he rebooted gcp-confluence-lv01 again and confirmed the database connections were working. The reboot moved past the error saw the previous night when trying to browse confluence.shrodinger.com . Later investigation of /var/log/syslog seems to show the initial issue was due to a stale PID file:

Jan 8 02:49:51 gcp-confluence-lv01 confluence1[1145]: Tomcat appears to still be running with PID 1334. Start aborted .

The new problem now seemed to be a timeout when trying to reach Confluence.

9:56am found the DNS for confluence.schrodinger.com was incorrect. NS1 had 35.196.139.50, but GCP had 34.74.141.80. found that gcp-confluence-lv01 had nic0 set to an Ephemeral IP.  That would explain why the IP address changed. updated the IP address for confluence.schrodinger.com in external DNS (NS1) to be 34.74.141.80. That fixed access to confluence.schrodinger.com from outside the network/not on Appgate. thought (mistakenly) that it wasn't yet working inside the network/on Appgate due to the 3600 TTL in external DNS.

12:48pm confluence.schrodinger.com was not yet resolving to 34.74.141.80 in the office or on Appgate, but sent a partial all-clear to the IT hotline and an email to all domain users, explaining this caveat.

4:25pm belatedly realized that we needed to update the IP address for confluence.schrodinger.com in internal DNS. Updated it on pdx-ad-wv01.

5:03pm confirmed confluence.schrodinger.com was now resolving to 34.74.141.80 in the office and on Appgate. Updated the ticket, messaged the IT hotline, and sent an email to all domain users.

11:58am added reserved IP addresses for gcp-confluence-lv01 (and gcp-jira-lv01).

Corrective Action

Another reboot of gcp-confluence-lv01

Updating the IP address for confluence.schrodinger.com to 34.74.141.80 in external DNS (nsone.net)

Updating the IP address for confluence.schrodinger.com to 34.74.141.80 in internal DNS (pdx-ad-wv01)

Assigning static IP addresses for gcp-confluence-lv01 (and gcp-jira-lv01) in GCP
