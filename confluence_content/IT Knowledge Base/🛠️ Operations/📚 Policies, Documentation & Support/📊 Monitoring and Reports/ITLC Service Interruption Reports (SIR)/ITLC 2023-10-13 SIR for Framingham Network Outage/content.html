Executive summary

On the Framingham office experienced an unplanned internet outage due to a fiber outage in the area by their Primary ISP (Astound/Upstream provider Crown).

FMA had a scheduled building maintenance power outage happening at the same time so we weren't sure if this was an affect of that or our ISP. (This was not communicated to IT and communication via the Core room was not clear.)

Background


The FMA's internet access is provided by Astound ( https://www.astound.com/ ) was down due to an fiber outage in the area , with a secondary ISP available via manual fail-over We failed over to our secondary Crown once we acknowledged it was an ISP related issue. ( https://business.comcast.com ).

Root Cause: Fiber Outage which was caused by a failed card on Crowns network equipment.

Incident Description

Astounds upstream provider Crown experienced a fiber outage in the Framingham Mass area.

Chronology

On  Friday at 7:45 AM was alerted of Packet loss in FMA.
mentioned an email about some repairs that was taking place in FMA, however this was not related to our broadband and had no affect on our internet in the physical office. (Our MDF did not loose power.)

noticed FMA was down at 8:58AM EST. and started to investigate. I pinged the ITINFRA room as I did not notice the conversation/Thread in the IT-Core room.

I confirmed this because I could not ping Crowns Gateway or the Transit interface IP address. I also noticed that all FMA devices were down in LibreNMS, and pagerduty was also a hard down for Crown.

I attempted to reach the FW from the secondary IP interface to further investigate but that was unreachable. (I thought maybe https was not still enabled on the Comcast interface but that was not the case. It was actually enabled.)

I Dm'd Via Gchat for assistance.

9:10 AM EST starts to investigate.

We could not reach the FW in FMA, so we needed someone physically onsite to assist.

At 9:20 AM EST we got on a call with and started walked him through how to failover to our secondary link.

We encountered a few issues in regards to locating the Admin credential folder in Lastpass for the FW which delayed the Failover by about 5 mins however once we located the credentials failover was seemless.

After failed over to Comcast at 9:52 AM EST. But did not update the Chat room until 10:12 AM EST.

Opened an Outage ticket for Astound shortly after failover. Case ID CS0068491 ( https://wave.service-now.com/ )

Astound opened a ticket with their type 2 provider to investigate at 12:47 PM EST.

Type 2 provider identified that this was related to a fiber outage in the Framingham Mass area.

I followed up Via email because we did not receive an update from our primary ISP but I was able to ping Astounds Gateway IP at 7:31 PM EST.

Astound responded at 8:39 PM EST to  and informed us that the issue is resolved and their tech is hands off.

We stayed on our secondary until Sunday morning and I failed back over to Astound at 11:03 AM EST Sunday.

Corrective Action

Static routes were adjusted on the Firewall in FMA. We made our Secondary link our Primary Link.

Analysis

External ISP failures led to a prolonged outage affecting nearly all FMA users/systems.

Since our secondary link wasn't fully configured access to other sites and resources was not accessible. EX Archive, GCP, AWS, Bolt. etc

Failing over to a secondary ISP was a manual process that required physical and configuration changes.

Detection

How quickly was the problem detected?

Fairly quickly by .

How was the problem detected?

PagerDuty, LibreNMS, Pingdom.

How can detection be improved?

I think detection worked well overall we have other areas we can improve in.

Response

How quickly did we respond once detected?

Around 25 - 30 minutes.

Did we adequately communicate to our users?

No we should have done this Via Chat an Via Email. Communication could have been improved.

If not, how can this be improved?

While one person was looking into this issue we should have coordinated with someone else to send out email and Chat communication to our end users.

It was a Friday so the office wasn't as occupied I assume that's why many users in HAM didn't acknowledge the issue.

Mitigation

What went well that helped minimize the impact of this problem?

Coordination, Team Work.

What can be done to minimize the impact if a problem like this recurs?

We need to test an automate automatic failover.

We need to make sure we have redundant tunnels for our secondary Link. Primary and Secondary link should be identical.

Communication could have been improved.


Avoidance

What can we do in the future to avoid a problem like this?

Redundant routers peering directly with our ISP's over BGP, removing the need for static default routes.
