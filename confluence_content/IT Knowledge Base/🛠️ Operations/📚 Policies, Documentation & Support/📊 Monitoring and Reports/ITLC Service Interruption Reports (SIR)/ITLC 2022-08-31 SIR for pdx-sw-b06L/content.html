Executive summary

Monitoring and management of pdx-sw-b06L stopped working.

Background

The ancient Blade switches serving the PDXGPU and test clusters in the Portland datacenter have begun to randomly stop responding and/or rebooting.

Incident Description

In this instance, the switch did not come back into service (from a management perspective).

Chronology

:

13:13 LibreNMS shows pdx-sw-b06L " Device status changed to Down from icmp check."
13:22 PagerDuty notifies and , Austin Acknowledges the alert and snoozes for 24h.

:

13:26 PagerDuty notifies , , and
13:31 ack's notification and confirms outage.
13:40 , , and join investigation.
14:20 After discovering that the console port is not providing output and there is no other way to reach the device, Nate B manually power cycles the switch. The switch boots normally and begins responding on the console port and over the network.

Corrective Action

Physical reboot of the switch was needed in order to restore service.

Analysis

These switches need to be replaced ASAP.

It's interesting to note that only management and monitoring were ever affected - the switch continued to perform as expected.

Detection

How quickly was the problem detected?

The problem was immediately detected.

How was the problem detected?

LibreNMS detected the outage and sent notifications to PagerDuty.

How can detection be improved?

Detection was sufficient in this instance.

Response

How quickly did we respond once detected?

It was more than 24h before anyone responded to the outage.

Did we adequately communicate to our users?

We are not aware of any impact to users, and no services were affected.

If not, how can this be improved?


Mitigation

What went well that helped minimize the impact of this problem?

The impact was, mysteriously, minimal.
As previously noted, the only services that were affected were the management and monitoring of this switch, none of the devices that were connected to it experienced an outage.

What can be done to minimize the impact if a problem like this recurs?

While it's not ideal, because cutting and restoring power to the switch was necessary to resume normal functionality, it would be a good idea to audit our network-connected PDUs to ensure that permanent devices (like switches) are correctly labeled in the PDU to allow for remote power cycling.
