Executive summary


An issue with routing resulted in a disruption to wireless at the San Deigo Swing space.

Background

Wireless relies on a connection between the AP's and the controller, hosted in GCP

Incident Description

There was an allow all rule in place, which resulted in some conflicting routes getting sent to GCP. We were not able to determine which specific route was causing the issues, due to a lack of troubleshooting tools on the GCP side.

The issue was resolved by removing the allow all rule in the outbound route map, and reconstructing community-list based advertisements similar to how other sites are configured.

Chronology

Early afternoon - was notified of the issue - it was resolved within an hour.

Corrective Action

The outbound route-maps were fixed.

Analysis

This appears to have been a mistake by someone on their way out - understandable.

Detection

How quickly was the problem detected?

Within an hour

How was the problem detected?

User reports

How can detection be improved?

Wireless client monitoring, like Ruckus Analytics

Response

How quickly did we respond once detected?

Immediately.

Did we adequately communicate to our users?

Site staff handled communication - due to this being a small spoke site, communication was straightforward.

Mitigation

What went well that helped minimize the impact of this problem?

Good overall BGP configuration outside of GCP (which has limited settings and lacks inbound route-maps)

What can be done to minimize the impact if a problem like this recurs?

Nothing really.

Avoidance

What can we do in the future to avoid a problem like this?

Automated testing for network changes.
