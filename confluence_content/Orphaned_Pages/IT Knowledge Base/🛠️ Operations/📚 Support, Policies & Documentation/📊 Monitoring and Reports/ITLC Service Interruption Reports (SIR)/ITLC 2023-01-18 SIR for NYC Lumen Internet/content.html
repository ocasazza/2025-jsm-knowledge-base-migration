Executive summary

Lumen experienced a Fiber break and Fan issues on January 18th 2023 in the early morning. This caused an internet outage in the NYC office due to Transport cards overheating in Lumens data center. The NYC office circuit was down.

Background

The NYC's internet access is provided by Lumen ( https://www.lumen.com/ ) was down , with a secondary ISP available via manual fail-over via GTT ( https://www.gtt.net ).

Root Cause: An equipment fault impacted unprotected services in New York, NY. Fix Action: Fan speed was increased to cool equipment.

Incident Description

NYC's internet access provided by Lumen ( https://www.lumen.com/ ), was impacted by a network fault. Manual fail-over to our secondary ISP via GTT ( https://www.gtt.net ) was required.

Chronology

All times are ET, unless otherwise noted:

2023-01-18

11:10 - User reports NYC office wifi down and Appgate not connecting to the NYC site. Other reports of an inability to connect to resources start coming in from other sites.

11:35 - Raghavendra f ailed NYC over to GTT, office wifi reported as being up and services starting to reconnect. Certain resources tied to static Lumen IPs are still unavailable.

16:02 - Lumen calls Nathaniel back to report that service should be back up

16:05 - Austin jumps in to re-enable Lumen and get tunnels back on the primary WAN

16:30 - Lumen back up and fully operational on nyc-fw, tunnels back on primary WAN. BGP functioning normally globally


Corrective Action

Static routes were adjusted to disable the Lumen connection, and were re-enabled post-outage.

Analysis

External failures led to a prolonged outage affecting nearly all users worldwide specifically anything specifically (NYC) . The process for recovering from those failures required skilled personnel and an advanced understanding of internal and external configurations and dependencies.

Failing over to a secondary ISP was a manual process that required physical and configuration changes that were not, and likely could not be, automated.

The impact of this outage will lead to large-scale changes in the way that systems interconnect and our response to failures.

Detection

How quickly was the problem detected?

Fairly quickly.

How was the problem detected?

Initially via user reports, monitoring caught up afterwards due to our delay settings.

How can detection be improved?

I think detection worked well overall

Response

How quickly did we respond once detected?

Around 25 minutes.

Did we adequately communicate to our users?

Yes, via email and the chat hotline.

Mitigation

What went well that helped minimize the impact of this problem?

Routing and policies in place for the GTT connection, some tunnel redundancy.

What can be done to minimize the impact if a problem like this recurs?

Better tunnel redundancy, creation of some runbooks for the ISP failover process.

Avoidance

What can we do in the future to avoid a problem like this?

Redundant routers peering directly with our ISP's over BGP, removing the need for static default routes.
