Executive summary

At approximately 07:09 AM EST, the Wireless Access Points (WAPS) within the Boston-Cambridge office experienced a loss of heartbeat and subsequently disconnected at 7:13 AM. This resulted in a complete outage of all WAPS in the Boston office, leading to the cessation of Wi-Fi services.

Upon receiving notification from an end user, Mati promptly verified the status of the WAPS through the Ruckus WebUi console. Following the confirmation of the WAPS outage, Mati informed Me, who undertook a thorough investigation of the issue. I subsequently verified the loss of Wi-Fi connectivity throughout the office.

Background

The Wi-Fi connectivity at the Cambridge office location in Boston was disrupted, adversely affecting day-to-day office operations.

Wifi connectivity is a day to day office requirement which must remain operational.

Incident Description

Wifi Down.

Chronology

(Times are in EST)

  • 7:09 - WAPS lost heartbeat.
  • 7:13 - WAPS Disconnected from Ruckus controller.
  • 8:53 - Mati is informed by Wade Miller of the Wifi being down in the Boston office.
  • 9:03 - Mati informed Nathaniel that the WAPS is down in Boston.
  • 9:09 - Nathaniel Jumped on a call with Mati and confirmed Wifi was down in BOS.
  • 9:12 - Mati confirms that all WIfi and Wired connections are inaccessible.
  • 9:13 - Nathaniel Williams was able to reach the Boston office FW and all devices in the Boston MDF, however Wifi and wired Lan connection to desktops were down.
  • 9:26 - Nathaniel Failed over to secondary ISP in BOS to see if the WAPS recover due to not finding any evidence as to why the WAPS were offline. All physical connected devices were accessible, ex Desktop, FW, Switches.
  • 9:27 - Wifi Recovers immediately after failover and Wifi is restored on Lumen ISP.
  • 9:43 - Sean Mcmanus and Raghavendra initiates investigation and takes a second look.
  • 11:00 - Sean opens support ticket with GTT to figure out why the WAPS went down.
  • GTT finds no issues on their end and recommend we power cycle the router.
  • Nathaniel and Sean Coordinate with Sekani to head over to the Cambridge Boston office to reboot.
  • 6:10PM - Sekani arrives onsite to power cycles the router but no luck. Sekani then reseated the cable at each end, GTT then was back up

Corrective Action

The corrective action was to reseat the cable on both ends between the router and FW.

Analysis

  • I, Nathaniel was able to reach the NYC FW via SSH and the GUI. Nathaniel also confirmed that all switches in the MDF was accessible.
  • I, Nathaniel, didn't confirm if bgp routes were still being advertised, this could have been looked into before failing over. Also validating that all tunnels were up without any potential issues.
